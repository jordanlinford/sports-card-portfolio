What matters structurally

You already have two different problems:

Problem	Best solution
Current leaderboard needs more context now	Add a fast label layer
Product needs a better long-term market model	Build tiering from your own data

So the right answer is not either/or. It is phased.

Best path
Phase 1: ship the simple label now

Add one descriptor per player market such as:

Example label	Meaning
Broad-based demand	Strength across the market
High-end led	Gains concentrated in premium segment
Low-end heavy	Retail-led move, weaker conviction
Mixed market	No clean alignment across segments
Exhaustion risk	Strength is narrow and fading

Why this is the right first move:

Benefit	Reason
Fast	No major UI rebuild
Improves trust	Explains why a player is ranked high
Helps Reddit defensibility	Gives you a cleaner narrative
Bridges to future tier model	Same concept, lighter implementation
Phase 2: build true 3-tier market segmentation from your own comp data

This is the strategic move.

Do not use fixed dollar tiers. Use percentile bands within each player market.

A clean starting model:

Tier	Suggested definition
Low/Base	bottom 60% of sales prices
Mid-tier	next 30%
High-end	top 10%

Then for each tier compute:

Metric	Purpose
Avg sale price trend	Direction
Sales count / velocity	Demand
Listings-to-sales ratio	Supply pressure
Volatility	Stability
Sell-through / liquidity proxy	Ease of exit

That gives you a real market structure view rather than one blended average.

Why this should come from your own data
Reason	Implication
You control methodology	More explainable
You can recompute historically	Better backtesting
You are not dependent on Gemini formatting	More stable pipeline
It becomes proprietary product logic	More defensible moat

Gemini can still help with:

card/entity normalization
comp grouping
narrative explanation

But the market structure math should be yours.

What I would not do

I would not jump straight to full 3-column leaderboard UI yet.

Why:

Risk	Why
UI complexity	Leaderboard gets harder to scan
Data immaturity	If tier logic is noisy, the UI exposes it
Misleading confidence	Fancy layout before strong tier math can hurt trust

First prove the tier model in the backend. Then expose it selectively.

Recommended sequence
Step	Action
1	Add a one-line market descriptor to leaderboard entries
2	Compute tier buckets from historical comp observations offline
3	Validate on 20–30 player markets you know well
4	Add a hidden/internal tier-debug view
5	Only then promote tier columns or expandable tier detail in UI
The actual product insight

Your leaderboard should answer two separate questions:

Question	Current answer	Better answer
Which player markets are strongest right now?	Score / phase / verdict	Keep this
Where inside that market is the strength happening?	Not answered	Add descriptor, then tiers

That second layer is where the system becomes hard to dismiss.
