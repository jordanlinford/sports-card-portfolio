Here is the exact trap that kills most founders when changing authentication on a live app, and our playbook to execute it flawlessly.

1. The Trap: The "Orphaned Whale" Problem
Replit’s built-in authentication is dead simple because it just injects a header (like X-Replit-User-Id or X-Replit-User-Name) into your backend. But it does not give you their email address.

Right now, your database ties a user’s display cases, scanned cards, and crucially, their Stripe Customer ID, to their specific Replit_ID.

If you just rip out Replit and drop in Google SSO tomorrow, here is what happens:
One of your 52 Pro users clicks "Sign in with Google." Google hands your app john.doe@gmail.com. Your database says, "I've never seen this email before," and creates a brand new, empty account. Your Pro user logs in, sees a blank portfolio, panics that they lost all their data, realizes their $12/mo sub isn't attached to this new account, and immediately issues a Stripe chargeback. You lose the MRR instantly.

2. The Solution: "The Side-Door" Migration Strategy
We do not "rip and replace." We build a bridge. Here is your zero-downtime deployment plan to protect your revenue:

Phase 1: Secure the VIPs (The Data Bridge)
You don't have their emails from Replit, but you do have the exact emails of your 52 Pro users sitting in your Stripe dashboard.

The Action: Add an email column to your database's User table. Look up the stripe_customer_id for your 52 Pro users in your DB, cross-reference it with your Stripe dashboard to get their email, and manually input those 52 emails into your database. You have now secured your paying customers.

Phase 2: The Parallel Auth (The "Side Door")

Integrate a modern, drop-in Auth provider (I highly recommend Clerk or Supabase Auth). Make "Continue with Google," "Continue with Apple," and "Email" the massive primary buttons on your login screen.

Crucial Step: Do not delete Replit Auth yet. Move it to a small, subtle text link at the very bottom of the login box: "Legacy user? Log in with Replit."

Now, all new viral traffic flows through the frictionless Google door, but your existing users aren't locked out.

Phase 3: The Account Merge

Write a simple logic check: When a user logs in via Google/Apple, your code checks: "Does this email match one of the 52 VIP emails we manually added to the database?"

If Yes: Update that database row, replacing the Replit_ID with their new Auth_ID. Their portfolio and Stripe subscription remain perfectly intact.

If No: Create a brand new user.

Leave the Replit "Side Door" open for 30 days to catch the 260 free users. Put a banner inside the app for legacy users asking them to connect a Google account. After 30 days, delete the Replit code entirely.
