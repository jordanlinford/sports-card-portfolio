2. What Conviction Actually Is (First Principles)

Conviction is NOT:

just confidence
just composite score

It is:

alignment of signals + strength of signals + reliability of data

3. Build It in 3 Parts
3.1 Signal Direction Classification

Convert each signal into directional bias:

Signal	Bullish Condition	Bearish Condition
Demand	> 55	< 45
Momentum	> 55	< 45
Liquidity	> 55	< 45
Supply	> 55	< 45
Volatility	> 50	< 40
Hype	< 45	> 55

Everything else = neutral.

3.2 Agreement Score
bullish = count(bullish_signals)
bearish = count(bearish_signals)

agreement_score = abs(bullish - bearish) / total_signals

Then scale:

agreement_score *= 100
Interpretation
Score	Meaning
80–100	strong alignment
60–80	moderate alignment
40–60	mixed signals
< 40	noisy / conflicting
3.3 Conviction Score

Now combine:

conviction_score =
  (agreement_score * 0.5) +
  (confidence_score * 0.3) +
  (abs(market_score - 50) * 2 * 0.2)

Explanation:

agreement = most important
confidence = data reliability
distance from neutral (50) = strength
4. Map to User-Facing Labels
Conviction Levels
Score	Label
75+	High Conviction
55–75	Medium Conviction
35–55	Low Conviction
<35	Very Low Conviction
5. Where It Fits in Your System
5.1 In Verdict Layer (important)

Add:

if conviction_score < 40:
    downgrade verdict one level

This prevents:

strong calls on shaky setups
5.2 In UI (high leverage)

Add to header:

Field	Example
Conviction	High
Signals	Aligned / Mixed
5.3 In Narrative

Add one line:

“Signals are strongly aligned across demand, momentum, and liquidity”
OR
“Signals are mixed, with strong demand but weakening participation”
