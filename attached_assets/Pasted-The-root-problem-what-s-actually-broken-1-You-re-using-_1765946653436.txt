The root problem (what’s actually broken)
1) You’re using one “Risk” number to represent 3 different things

Liquidity risk (hard to sell fast)

Pricing uncertainty (wide comp dispersion / wrong matches)

Downside risk (likely to lose value)

For vintage cards:

Downside risk is usually low

Liquidity might be medium

Pricing uncertainty can be high if comps are messy

Your UI labels all of that as Risk, so collectors assume “this card is dangerous.”

That’s why it feels wrong.

Fix strategy (do this, in this order)
Step 1 — Split “Risk” into two signals

This one change will make the whole system feel sane.

Replace Risk with:

Downside Risk (1–10)
“How likely is value to drop significantly in the chosen horizon?”

Market Friction (1–10) (or “Liquidity”)
“How hard is it to buy/sell at a fair price quickly?”

Now a Pete Maravich PSA 8 becomes:

Downside Risk: 2/10

Market Friction: 6/10 (not many trades, pricing spread)

That matches reality.

Step 2 — Add “Card Match Confidence” (separate from Data Confidence)

Your range ($39–$300) screams: matching problem.

Add:

Card Match Confidence: High / Med / Low

Market Data Confidence: High / Med / Low

If match confidence is low, you should:

refuse to produce a clean “est value”

OR show “Needs confirmation” and force WATCH

This is the single best trust-builder you can add.

Step 3 — Redefine WATCH (it’s currently ambiguous)

Right now WATCH implies “don’t touch.”

For stable vintage, WATCH should mean:

“Stable asset, limited upside — track casually.”

You need WATCH reasons.

WATCH should map to one of these types:

WATCH: Stable / Low Upside

WATCH: Data Uncertain

WATCH: Waiting for catalyst

WATCH: Overheated

Same action, different meaning.

Make the UI show the reason.

Step 4 — Fix the price range logic (this is causing chaos)

Your range is likely including:

wrong grades

wrong parallels

raw listings

“similar” cards

For graded vintage, you should compute:

Matched Comps (strict): PSA 8 only

Neighbor Comps (loose): PSA 7–9 (optional)

And display:

“PSA 8 comps: $X–$Y (n=__)”

optionally “Broader comps: $A–$B”

Do NOT mix them in one range.
