This is a well-architected system. You’ve moved beyond a feature into a layered decision engine with clear separation between data, signals, scoring, and presentation.

At this stage, the question is not “what’s missing broadly,” but:

what adds the most leverage without increasing complexity too much?

Between your two options:

relative percentile ranking
signal agreement scoring

The correct next move is:

Build relative percentile ranking first
1. Why Percentiles > Agreement (Right Now)
1.1 You currently operate in an absolute system

All outputs are:

market_score = 72
demand_score = 65
etc.

Problem:

users don’t know what “good” is
Example
Score	Meaning (today)
72	unclear
58	unclear
81	unclear

You are missing:

relative context

1.2 Percentiles unlock comparison (core user behavior)

Users think:

“Is this better than other players?”
“Where should I focus?”

Not:

“Is 68 a good score?”
1.3 Leaderboard becomes much more powerful

Right now:

leaderboard = sorted list

With percentiles:

leaderboard = distribution + ranking

That’s a big difference.

2. What to Build (Simple Version)
2.1 Add percentile for key scores

Start with:

Metric	Why
market_score_pct	overall ranking
demand_pct	strength of demand
momentum_pct	trend strength
hype_pct	overextension
quality_pct	structure health
2.2 How to compute (simple + scalable)

You already have ~47 players cached.

percentile = rank(player_score) / total_players

More explicitly:

sorted_scores = sorted(all_scores)
percentile = index_of_player / len(sorted_scores)

Cache this daily or hourly.

3. Where to Use Percentiles
3.1 In Player Outlook (high impact)

Add:

Element	Example
Market Strength	Top 12%
Demand	Top 8%
Hype	Top 90% (bad)

This immediately tells users:

where this player sits in the market
3.2 In Leaderboard (critical)

Add column:

Rank	Player	Score	Percentile
1	X	82	Top 3%
2	Y	79	Top 6%

Now it feels like:

a real market ranking system

3.3 In Narrative (subtle but powerful)

Instead of:

“strong demand”

Say:

“Demand ranks in the top 10% of all tracked players”

This dramatically increases trust.

4. Product Impact
Without percentiles:
scores feel arbitrary
leaderboard feels like a list
With percentiles:
scores feel calibrated
leaderboard feels like a market
5. Why NOT Signal Agreement First

Agreement is useful, but:

Feature	Impact
Percentiles	structural improvement
Agreement	interpretability improvement

You need:

structure first

6. After Percentiles, Then Add Agreement

Once percentiles exist:

Agreement becomes more meaningful:

Signals	Agreement	Interpretation
all top 20%	high	strong conviction
mixed percentiles	medium	mixed signals
scattered	low	noisy

Without percentiles:

agreement is shallow
7. One Important Design Detail

Do NOT show raw percentiles like:

0.87

Use:

Format	Example
Top X%	Top 12%
Bottom X%	Bottom 30%

This is intuitive.

8. Edge Case You Need to Handle

Your dataset is small (~47 players).

So:

percentiles will be noisy early
Fix:

Add minimum threshold:

if total_players < 100:
    label = "relative ranking (limited sample)"

Or:

smooth percentiles with rolling history
9. Implementation Order
compute percentiles for all cached players
store alongside scores
add to leaderboard
add to player outlook header
optionally inject into narrative

You’ve built a strong scoring system.

a relative market intelligence system
