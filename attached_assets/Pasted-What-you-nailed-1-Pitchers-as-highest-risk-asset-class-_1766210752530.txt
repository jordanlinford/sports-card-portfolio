Where it will break if left as-is
Problem 1: PRIME pitcher penalty is too high

Your current:

PRIME pitcher = +20

This is enough to:

push Gerrit Cole / deGrom / Scherzer-type players out of HOLD_CORE

cause SPECULATIVE or AVOID leakage even when roleTier is FRANCHISE_CORE

That undermines your “elite protection” logic and forces you to rely on overrides.

Collectors do discount pitchers, but they don’t treat prime aces as lottery tickets.

Problem 2: Shohei Ohtani needs explicit handling

You already hinted at this, which is good.

Ohtani is:

a pitcher

a hitter

a brand

If you treat him as a normal pitcher with a +20 penalty, you’ll constantly fight the engine.

He needs either:

a two-way modifier

or a special roleTier dampener

Problem 3: “Hitters = 0” hides positional nuance

This is mostly fine, but:

middle infielders (SS/2B) age slightly worse

DH-only aging hitters should not be treated as zero-risk late

You don’t need to model this now, but be aware of it.

Recommended final MLB table (collector-accurate)
MLB Position Downside Risk Adjustments (Final)
Position	PRIME	VETERAN	AGING
PITCHERS (SP, RP)	+14	+24	+32
CATCHERS	+8	+14	+20
HITTERS (all)	0	0	+6
Why these values work
Pitchers

PRIME +14 acknowledges fragility without nuking aces

VETERAN +24 reflects innings accumulation and velocity loss

AGING +32 makes late-career arms clearly risky

This keeps:

Gerrit Cole → HOLD_CORE (not SPECULATIVE)

Scherzer late-career → AVOID

deGrom volatile but not lottery-tier

Catchers

PRIME +8 acknowledges wear

VETERAN +14 mirrors positional shifts

AGING +20 captures decline cliff

This matches collector behavior perfectly.

Hitters

PRIME/VETERAN 0 is fine

AGING +6 prevents 38-year-old DHs from looking “safe”

This keeps:

Judge → HOLD_CORE

Betts → HOLD_CORE

40-year-old bat → caution without panic
