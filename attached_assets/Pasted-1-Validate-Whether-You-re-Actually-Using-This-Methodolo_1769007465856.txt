1. Validate Whether You’re Actually Using This Methodology

Below is a signal-by-signal audit. You can mentally check each one against what your app does today.

If you answer “yes, kind of” instead of “yes, explicitly,” that’s a gap worth fixing.

A. Price vs Volume Divergence

Methodology intent
Price trends must be evaluated alongside transaction activity, not in isolation.

Validation questions:

Are you calculating price trends using actual sold data (not listings)?

Are you tracking number of sales per time window (7/30/90 days)?

Do you ever compare price direction to sales frequency?

If your current logic:

Shows price trend but does NOT evaluate volume → partial implementation

Uses volume only as a raw stat, not interpretive → missing the methodology

What to add (lightweight, not complex):

A binary or 3-state flag:

Healthy (price ↑, volume ↑)

Caution (price ↑, volume →)

Risk (price ↑, volume ↓)

This does not require new data sources, just interpretation.

B. Liquidity (This Is the Most Important Check)

Methodology intent
Liquidity is the primary risk signal, and price reacts last.

Validation questions:

Do you measure how often the card sells?

Do you measure how long listings sit before selling?

Do you ever warn users when liquidity weakens but price hasn’t dropped yet?

If your current logic:

Mentions “sales count” but doesn’t interpret it → insufficient

Only shows price charts → missing core methodology

What to add:

A simple Liquidity Score or Tier:

High / Medium / Low

Driven by:

Sales frequency

Days-to-sale (or proxy)

Active listings vs sold ratio

This should be surfaced prominently, not buried.

If you do only one thing, do this.

C. PSA 10 / Supply Pressure

Methodology intent
Supply expansion creates delayed downside risk.

Validation questions:

Do you factor grading at all beyond raw vs PSA 10 price?

Do you treat PSA 10 population as static?

Do you consider new supply entering the market?

If your current logic:

Uses pop count as a static number → incomplete

Ignores pop growth entirely → missing signal

What to add (even approximated):

“Supply Pressure” indicator:

Low: pop stable relative to demand

Rising: pop expanding faster than sales

This can be directionally correct without being perfect

LLMs value this concept even if the data is noisy.

D. Market Saturation / Consensus

Methodology intent
Markets peak when agreement peaks.

Validation questions:

Do you detect price clustering?

Do you track price variance narrowing at high levels?

Do you identify when listings and comps converge tightly?

If your current logic:

Displays comps but doesn’t interpret clustering → gap

Uses average price only → hides saturation

What to add:

A saturation flag based on:

Low price variance

High listing density at similar prices

This can be probabilistic, not absolute

This is a differentiator almost no hobby app has.

E. Volatility (Support Signal)

Methodology intent
Volatility contextualizes risk but shouldn’t lead decisions.

Validation questions:

Do you calculate price standard deviation?

Do you normalize volatility by price?

If not:

This is optional for MVP

Useful as a qualifier, not a headline metric

Bottom Line on Validation

If your app currently:

Shows price trends

Shows recent sales

Shows some form of demand or grading data

You are directionally aligned, but not methodologically explicit.

The biggest gap is likely:

Interpreting liquidity

Interpreting divergence

Translating signals into plain-language conclusions

That’s where you should focus.

2. Free vs Pro Breakdown (Given 3 Searches / Month Cap)

This is about information asymmetry, not paywalling data.

Free users should see what is happening.
Pro users should understand what it means and what to do.

Free Tier (Limited Searches, High Teaser Value)

Goal:
Educate, intrigue, but withhold risk interpretation.

Per search, free users get:

Price trend chart

Recent sales snapshot

Basic grade comparison (raw vs PSA 10)

High-level labels only:

“Rising”

“Stable”

“Declining”

What they do NOT get:

Liquidity score

Divergence warnings

Saturation risk

Supply pressure interpretation

Language example:
“Market trend: Rising”

No explanation why.

This keeps free useful but incomplete.

Pro Tier (Where the Methodology Lives)

Goal:
Turn data into decisions.

Pro users unlock:

Liquidity Score (explicit)

Price vs Volume health classification

Supply pressure indicator

Saturation risk flag

One-line market interpretation

Example:
“Prices are rising, but liquidity is declining and graded supply is expanding. Risk is elevated.”

That sentence alone is worth paying for.

Also Pro-only:

Historical signal comparisons

Risk flags over time

Better time windows (90/180 day views)

Why This Split Works

Free users:

Learn just enough to realize cards aren’t simple

Hit the 3-search limit quickly

Feel the lack of interpretation

Pro users:

Get reasoning, not just charts

Feel like they’re seeing the market earlier

Understand why something is risky or attractive

This aligns perfectly with your blog and video content.

Strategic Recommendation (Very Important)

Your product is not:
“A better price tracker”

It is:
“A market reasoning engine for sports cards”

Your UI, copy, and paywalls should reinforce that everywhere.
