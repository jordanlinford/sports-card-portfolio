If almost every product is showing a positive return, the model is currently a "hype engine," not an investment tool. In the current market, 80-90% of sealed wax is a net-loss proposition the moment the shrink wrap is torn.
The math is likely failing because it’s capturing "Asking Prices" or "Ceiling Outcomes" rather than "Realized Liquidity."
Here is the direct diagnostic and the required fixes to bring the EV back to reality:
1. The "Survivor Bias" Error
Your current Step 1 is likely pulling data for the best cards (the "Key Hits") and multiplying them by their odds, but it is ignoring the Value Floor.
• The Problem: Most packs contain "base" or "low-end inserts" that have a market value of near-zero ($0.05 - $0.25). If your EV calculation ignores the 95% of the box that is "trash," the math stays artificially high.
• The Fix: You must factor in Transaction Friction. Any card with a market value under $5.00 should be treated as $0 in the EV calculation. Why? Because after eBay fees, shipping, and time, you cannot reliably liquidate it for a profit.
2. The "Mean vs. Median" Trap
If a box has one $10,000 "case hit" and 999 boxes have $20 worth of cards, the Mean EV looks great, but the Median experience is a total loss.
• The Problem: Using a simple average of hit values overstates the reality for the average buyer.
• The Fix: Switch to a Weighted Median EV. Heavily discount "one-of-one" or "super-short-print" (SSP) values in the general EV. Those should contribute to the Hit Ceiling Score, not the EV Ratio. The EV Ratio should represent what a user is likely to pull, not what they dream of pulling.
3. "Asking" vs. "Sold" Data
• The Problem: Your data source might be scraping active listings. Sellers can ask $100 for a card all day; it doesn't mean it sells.
• The Fix: Tighten the API parameters to Last 5 Comp Sales (Sold/Completed) only. If the system can't find 3+ sold comps in the last 90 days, the value of that hit must be aggressively hair-cut (by 50%+) due to illiquidity.
4. Adjust the EV Ratio Thresholds
Your current "Positive EV" threshold is too low.
• Current: Above 1.10x → Positive.
• Reality: Because of the 13-15% selling fees on platforms like eBay/COMC, a 1.10x return is actually a Break Even at best.
• New Proposed Thresholds:
• Negative EV: Below 0.95x
• Speculative/Neutral: 0.95x to 1.25x
• Positive EV: Above 1.25x (This is the "Black Swan" territory where the box is actually undervalued).
5. The "Bulk" Fallacy
Are you counting the value of "Base Rookies"?
• The Correction: In the "Junk Slabs" era, base rookies of even top players (Caleb Williams, Victor Wembanyama) have seen massive supply spikes. Unless it's a high-end brand (e.g., Prizm, Optic, Chrome) and the card is likely to gem, base paper cards should carry $0 weight in a professional ROI model.
