Good — that was the hard part. If you’ve already aligned the Card Outlook copy with the Hidden Gems voice, you’ve fixed 70% of the problem. What’s left is not wording polish; it’s structural and conceptual.

Here’s what still needs to happen for Card Outlook to fully “lock in” as a trusted collector tool.

1. CARD OUTLOOK NEEDS A CLEAR JOB

Right now, Card Outlook is doing too many things at once:

• explaining the card
• scoring the card
• giving a recommendation
• disclaiming responsibility

It needs a single, dominant purpose.

What it should be:

Card Outlook answers one question only:
“What is the market currently saying about this specific card, and how should I treat it?”

That means:

Not portfolio strategy
Not discovery (Hidden Gems)
Not shopping (Next Buys)

Just: interpretation.

If you keep this mental boundary, everything else becomes easier.

2. REMOVE ABSTRACT SCORES AS THE PRIMARY OUTPUT

Even with better language, raw numbers like:

Upside: 45
Risk: 34
Confidence: 65

are still doing too much cognitive damage.

Collectors don’t think in numbers at the card level. They think in states.

What needs to happen:

• Scores become supporting evidence, not the headline
• The headline is a state-based verdict

Example structure:

Card Outlook
Current Market State: WATCH
Why: Prices have risen, but demand is uneven and volume is thin.

Then show:
– Upside (translated: Moderate)
– Risk (translated: Low–Moderate)
– Confidence (translated: Medium)

Numbers can stay — but visually secondary.

3. CARD OUTLOOK MUST BE TIME-AWARE

This is the single biggest credibility upgrade you can make.

Right now, Card Outlook feels static.
Markets are not.

What needs to happen:

Add an explicit “time context” line.

Examples:
• “This outlook reflects current market behavior over the last 30–60 days.”
• “Short-term outlook; re-evaluate after playoffs.”
• “Long-term outlook; near-term volatility expected.”

This does two things:

Protects you when conditions change

Teaches collectors how often to revisit decisions

Without this, even good analysis will eventually feel wrong.

4. CARD OUTLOOK NEEDS CONDITIONAL TRIGGERS

This is where it becomes genuinely powerful.

Every Card Outlook should implicitly answer:
“What would make this change?”

You already do this in Hidden Gems. Card Outlook needs the lighter version.

Add a small section:

What Would Change This Outlook?
– Sustained weekly sales above $X
– Playoff performance
– Increase in graded volume
– Narrative shift (injury, role change, awards)

This turns the card from “information” into “monitoring.”

5. ALIGN CARD OUTLOOK WITH ACTION STATES

Right now you have WATCH, BUY, MONITOR concepts spread across the app.
Card Outlook should be the authoritative source for a card’s state.

That means:

Card Outlook verdict should directly drive:
• Watchlist inclusion
• Portfolio flags
• Next Buys eligibility
• Hidden Gems exclusion (if fully priced)

If a card is WATCH here, it should not feel like a BUY anywhere else without explanation.

This alignment is what makes the system feel intentional instead of layered.

6. CARD OUTLOOK SHOULD TEACH, NOT JUST REPORT

This is subtle but important.

Each Card Outlook should leave the user knowing something they didn’t before about how the hobby works.

Examples:
• “PSA 10s with wide price bands usually mean eye appeal is driving outcomes.”
• “Elite players without a signature moment often trade flat until a narrative breaks.”
• “Cards tied to playoff outcomes reprice faster than regular-season performance.”

One sentence. Not every time. But often enough that users learn.

That’s how trust compounds.

7. REMOVE REDUNDANT DISCLAIMERS, KEEP ONE STRONG ONE

You don’t need to keep reminding users this isn’t financial advice.

One quiet, consistent disclaimer at the bottom is enough.

Over-disclaiming weakens perceived confidence.

THE END STATE (WHAT “DONE” LOOKS LIKE)

When Card Outlook is done, it should feel like this:

• Fast to read (10–15 seconds)
• Clear state (Buy / Watch / Avoid / Hold)
• Clear reason (market behavior, not stats)
• Clear monitoring logic
• Consistent with every other feature

At that point, Card Outlook becomes the anchor feature — not Hidden Gems.

Hidden Gems excites.
Next Buys motivates.
Portfolio Outlook contextualizes.

Card Outlook explains.

BLUNT SUMMARY

You already fixed the language. Good.

What’s left:
• Make Card Outlook state-based, not score-based
• Add time context
• Add conditional triggers
• Align it as the single source of truth for card status

Do that, and Card Outlook stops feeling like “AI analysis” and starts feeling like “how a smart collector reads the market.”

That’s the difference between impressive and indispensable.
