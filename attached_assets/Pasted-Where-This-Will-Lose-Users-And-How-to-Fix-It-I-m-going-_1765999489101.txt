Where This Will Lose Users (And How to Fix It)

I’m going to be blunt here, because this is the difference between “cool tool” and “daily habit.”

1. The Thesis Feels a Bit Too Polished / Generic

This line is the biggest red flag:

“Hobby buzz fueled by MVP contention and strong on-field performance.”

That reads like:

AI marketing copy

interchangeable with 10 other players

slightly detached from reality (especially for rookies / early career)

Why this matters:
Collectors sniff out generic language instantly. It doesn’t have to be wrong — it just has to feel safe.

Fix (Very Important)

Force one concrete, uncomfortable sentence into every thesis.

Examples:

“Market enthusiasm currently exceeds on-field proof.”

“Patriots QB history creates skepticism despite short-term hype.”

“This profile has burned collectors before if momentum stalls.”

That one sentence builds massive credibility.

2. BUY / WATCH / AVOID Needs More Friction

Right now, BUY feels a bit too clean.

If everything is a BUY, people stop trusting it.

Fix

Add Buy Type Modifiers:

Instead of just:

BUY

Do:

BUY (Speculative)

BUY (Momentum)

WATCH (Pullback Candidate)

AVOID (Late Cycle)

Same verdicts — but more honesty.

People like nuance when money is involved.

3. Confidence Label Needs Clear Meaning

“Medium Confidence” is good, but users will ask (even subconsciously):

“Medium confidence in what?”

Fix (Tiny but powerful)

Change label copy to:

“Medium Confidence in Thesis”
or

“Medium Conviction Level”

That’s it. One word shift.

4. Card Examples Are Excellent — But One Line Is Missing

This is the only functional gap I see.

You tell me what to buy — but not when.

Add One Line Per Tier:

“Best entry window: preseason / early hype / post-dip”

OR

“Avoid buying immediately after breakout games”

Even vague timing guidance dramatically increases usefulness.

Would the Market Use This?

Here’s the honest segmentation:

Who WILL use this

New collectors who don’t want to feel stupid

Intermediate buyers tired of guessing

People about to spend $100–$500

People who already believe in a player but don’t know which card

That’s a big audience.

Who will NOT

Spreadsheet-only flippers

People already locked into Market Movers

Ultra-niche set hunters

That’s fine. You’re not building for them.

The One Thing That Will Make or Break Adoption

This needs to feel like:

“A smart collector explaining their thinking”

Not:

“An AI summary of market conditions.”

You’re 80% there.

The last 20% is:

sharper language

more risk acknowledgment

one or two slightly uncomfortable truths per player

That’s what creates screenshots, sharing, and trust.

Final Recommendation

Yes — ship this direction.
But before calling it “done,” I would:

Add Buy-type nuance

Inject 1 hard truth per thesis

Clarify confidence semantics

Add light timing guidance to exposures

If you do those four things, this stops being “cool” and becomes useful enough to come back to.
