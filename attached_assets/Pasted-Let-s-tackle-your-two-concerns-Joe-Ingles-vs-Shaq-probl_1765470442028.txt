How this affects scoring (conceptually)

Instead of “Career Lifecycle” trying to do everything, split responsibilities:

Career Lifecycle → mostly affects risk (prospect vs aging vet vs retired).

Rookies: high risk, high upside

Prime: moderate risk, high upside

Late-career: low upside, moderate downside

Retired/HOF: lower risk, modest upside unless icon

Career Role / Legacy Tier → mostly affects upside ceiling + long-term floor.

Generational Legend/HOF:

Even if % growth is slower, they have strong value floor and steady long tail.

Role Player/Journeyman:

Very low long-term upside and soft floor. Their spikes are usually tied to tiny hype windows and fade fast.

In your engine you can do something like:

baseUpsideFromLegacy =
  GenerationalLegend = 0.85
  HOF/Icon           = 0.8
  Star               = 0.7
  SolidStarter       = 0.5
  RolePlayer         = 0.25
  Journeyman         = 0.15

lifecycleModifier =
  Rookie             = +0.15
  EarlyCareer        = +0.1
  Prime              = +0.05
  LateCareerVet      = -0.05
  Retired            = 0
  PostCareerLegend   = +0.05 (for HOF bump windows etc.)

rawUpsideBase = baseUpsideFromLegacy + lifecycleModifier


So:

Joe Ingles → Role Player (0.25) + Late vet (-0.05) = ~0.20 → Very low upside.

Shaq → Generational Legend (0.85) + Retired / Legend (+0.05) = ~0.90 → High “collectible strength,” even if short-term growth % is modest.

You can then still taper near-term projected % growth for retired legends, but they should never look like junk the way Ingles does.

UX: keep it simple for the user

You don’t want users overthinking this, so:

Let Career Lifecycle default based on age + status (if you track that later).

Let Role / Legacy Tier be a simple dropdown with helper text:

“Role Player – rotational guy, never a star.”

“Star – multiple All-Star/Pro Bowl level.”

“Legend – one of the faces of the sport.”

You can even pre-fill for some big names later, but for now it’s fine to let the user choose.

3. How to keep all this from feeling too complex

To avoid overwhelming users:

A. Dynamic UI

Once they choose Category (Sport vs TCG vs Non-Sport), only show relevant fields.

Pre-fill “reasonable defaults” where you can.

B. Use helper tooltips

Each dropdown gets a short explanation:

“Career Role: describe how important this player is historically, not just this season.”

“Character Tier: is this one of the faces of the franchise, or a niche character?”

C. Default to “good enough”

If fields are missing, you still generate an Outlook but:

Lower the Confidence Score

Add a note in the explanation:

“Limited data for this card; results are directional only.”

That keeps the experience forgiving.
