The Core Problem

Your login flow is missing one required OAuth parameter:

prompt=select_account


Without it:

Replit will always auto-login the currently authenticated user

You cannot test new users

It will feel “locked” to your account

The Fix (This Is the Important Part)
Step 1: Update your Replit OAuth URL

Wherever you build your Replit auth URL (server or client), update it to include:

&prompt=select_account

Example (generic)

Before:

https://replit.com/auth/oauth/authorize?
client_id=YOUR_CLIENT_ID
&redirect_uri=YOUR_REDIRECT
&response_type=code
&scope=identity email profile


After:

https://replit.com/auth/oauth/authorize?
client_id=YOUR_CLIENT_ID
&redirect_uri=YOUR_REDIRECT
&response_type=code
&scope=identity email profile
&prompt=select_account


This forces the account chooser every time, even if you’re logged in.

Step 2: Clear Existing Session (Important)

After making the change, do one of the following:

Option A (fastest):

Open an incognito window

Visit your app

Try login

Option B (clean):

Log out of Replit entirely

Clear cookies for replit.com

Restart browser

Step 3: Confirm You Are NOT Hardcoding Owner Logic

Double-check you are not doing any of these:

Auto-creating user records with a fixed email

Overwriting user.email with env values

Treating the first user as “admin” and blocking others

Your screenshot suggests this is OAuth-level, not app logic — but it’s worth confirming.

How You Know It’s Fixed

When working correctly:

Clicking “Login” always shows a Replit account selector

You can choose:

a different Replit account

a brand new Replit user

New users get unique emails + IDs in your database

Important Production Note (Future You Will Thank You)

Replit Auth is great for dev/testing, but not ideal for public SaaS.

Before public launch, you should strongly consider:

Clerk

Firebase Auth

Auth0

Replit Auth is best for:

internal tools

early previews

demos

You’re fine for now — just don’t ship this publicly long-term.
