On the two flags:
Concourse — scope it tighter. Base Concourse really is just the foundation tier in Select, it's $1-5 territory for most players. A no-name Concourse base with zero comps isn't rare, it's just low-demand, and "too new — no verified sales" would actively mislead. Safer to only match Concourse when it's paired with a color/parallel word (Concourse Red, Blue, Gold, Tie-Dye, Prizm, Silver, Disco, etc.) — or honestly, drop Concourse from the pattern list entirely and let a general colored-Prizm pattern handle the parallels.
Negative — tighten it. The risk is low in practice but the fix is basically free, and you get zero false-positive surface area. Require "refractor" or "prizm" immediately after. "Negative Refractor" is the real Bowman Chrome thing you're trying to catch anyway.
On additions — in rough priority order of what I'd add:
Definitely worth it: Topps Heritage SP / High Number SP (classic Topps chase, notoriously hard to comp on release), Stained Glass (genuine chase insert), Image Variation / Photo Variation (photo SPs in flagship and Chrome are a huge chase category and users often have no idea which card they pulled), Shimmer (already? worth double-checking — both regular and FOTL).
Probably worth it: Mojo Refractor (Bowman Chrome), Atomic Refractor (Bowman Chrome), Downtown / Kaboom / Courtside / Color Blast (Panini inserts — some may already be covered, worth a pass), Bowman Chrome Gold /50 (would fall through your ≤25 gate but is absolutely a chase).
Lower priority: Rainbow Foil (Topps, common enough that no-comp usually means low-demand not rare), SSP as a literal string match (overlaps with a lot of your existing patterns).
One pattern I'd seriously consider adding that isn't on your list: a generic "colored Prizm/Refractor with named color" pattern — something like (silver|gold|red|blue|green|orange|purple|pink|teal|black|white)\s+(prizm|refractor|cracked ice|wave|mosaic). That catches the long tail of colored parallels across Panini and Topps without you having to enumerate every set. You'd want to gate it on compCount === 0 and exclude common non-numbered color variants, but it covers a lot of ground with one rule.
One thing I wouldn't add: anything requiring the word "rare" or "chase" in the title. Those are seller-added terms, not card metadata, and the false-positive rate on listing language is ugly.
